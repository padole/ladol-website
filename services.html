Berthing and vessel Handling

LADOL is registered as an ISPS-certified port facility with IMO, and receives local and international vessels. Featuring a 200m quay, 8.5m draft, 25-ton/m2 high load bearing area and additional 30-ton bollards at either end, LADOLâ€™s quay can accommodate up to six supply vessels and three Heavy Lift Vessels simultaneously.

The berthing crew works 24 hours a day and, since products are piped directly through ducts in the quay, vessel turnaround time can be less than 8 hours.

Due to market demand the quay is being extended by an additional 450m in 2012/13.

Secure, Modern Closed and Open Fabrigation/Storage Areas

All our storage areas are built to international standards and suitable for both storage and small fabrication works. These areas can also be extensively customized for project work and specialized storage, such as overhead cranes, racking, bund walls and epoxy floors, etc..

Purpose build facilities

LADOL is the first and only base specifically designed to meet the logistical needs of deep water offshore oil and gas operations and projects in and around Nigeria. As the first and only facility of its kind in Lagos, LADOL was created to ensure that the nation of Nigeria benefits directly from its deep offshore oil and gas reserves. By providing world-class facilities and services, LADOL will encourage the multinational firms exploiting the deep water oil reserves to utilize Nigerian companies to service their operations, creating jobs for Nigerians as well as ensuring training, knowledge and technology transfer into the country.
